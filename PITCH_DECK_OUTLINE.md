## Slide 2: The Problem

**Independent creators are trapped in a broken stack.**

Three columns:

| AI Tools Are Extractive | The Stack Is Fragmented | IP Is Gatekept |
|------------------------|------------------------|----------------|
| Black-box AI claims ownership of outputs | Artists need 8–12 disconnected tools to go from idea to revenue | Trademark, copyright, and licensing infrastructure costs thousands |
| Subscription models extract, don't return | Every integration is a friction point | Legal strategy is inaccessible to solo creators |
| No transparency on how models use your data | No portability — platforms own your workflow | Artists create value they can't protect |

**Bottom line:** Billions in creative output flows through systems designed to extract from creators, not empower them.

**Speaker note:** "If you're an independent artist today, you're paying for a DAW, an AI tool, a distributor, an education platform, and a lawyer — none of which talk to each other. Every one takes a cut. And none of them protect your IP. We're solving this."

**Multi-model AI orchestration, not a single-vendor wrapper.**

| Model | Role |
|-------|------|

Key differentiators:
- Standardised prompt templates across all workflows
- Context management and session persistence
- Task routing — right model for the right job
- Not locked to any single AI provider

**Speaker note:** "CoreeyAI isn't ChatGPT with a skin on it. It's a purpose-built orchestration layer that routes creative tasks to the best model for the job. Claude handles reasoning. Grok handles real-time data. Perplexity handles research. Suno generates audio. The system is modular — if a better model emerges tomorrow, we swap it in."
